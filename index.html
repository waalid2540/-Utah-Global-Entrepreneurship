The History of Global Entrepreneurship Week

From ancient trade to modern innovation - entrepreneurship has driven human progress for millennia

17000 B.C.

The First Entrepreneurs

New Guinea hunter-gatherers traded obsidian for essential and non-essential goods - the birth of entrepreneurship.

10000 B.C.

Agricultural Revolution

Domestication of animals and plants led to specialized economic niches and the first trading communities. The Fertile Crescent and cities like Uruk emerged as entrepreneurial hubs.

650 B.C.

Invention of Money

The creation of currency transformed society from barter systems to trust-based economies, accelerating entrepreneurial activity worldwide.

1800s

Industrial Revolution

Machines revolutionized economic development. Capitalism flourished, producing legendary entrepreneurs like Andrew Carnegie, John D. Rockefeller, and Henry Ford.

2008

🌍 Global Entrepreneurship Week Born

The Ewing Marion Kauffman Foundation created Global Entrepreneurship Week to celebrate the importance of entrepreneurship to global economic advancement and human welfare.

TODAY

Global Movement

From the U.S. and U.K. to over 165 countries worldwide - GEW has become the largest celebration of entrepreneurs and innovators on the planet.

🏔️ Utah Joins the Global Movement

As technologies evolve and entrepreneurship becomes the pillar of global economies, Salt Lake City proudly hosts entrepreneurs from every industry - continuing this 19,000-year legacy of innovation and trade.

No, you can attend individual sessions based on your interests and schedule.
